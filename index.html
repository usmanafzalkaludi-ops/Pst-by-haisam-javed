The Teacher

A historian's voice. An analyst's eye.

Haisam Javed is an educationist and a data scientist — an unusual mix that shapes how he teaches. History isn't a pile of dates in his classroom; it's cause and effect. Geography isn't map-colouring; it's patterns in rainfall, rivers and trade you learn to read like data.

For over a decade he has taught O Level and IGCSE Pakistan Studies at reputable institutes across Karachi, building a reputation for making the 2059 syllabus feel less like a textbook and more like the story of a country.

The Syllabus, As A Journey

Scroll through 200 years of Pakistan.

Twelve stops. From the decline of the Mughals to the power stations on the Indus — the full 2059 route, in order.

History · 01

1857 — The War of Independence

Why the uprising failed, what the British changed after it, and how religious thinkers like Shah Waliullah set the stage long before the first shot.

History · 02

Sir Syed & the Aligarh Movement

Education as resistance. How one man's university shaped the political vocabulary of South Asian Muslims for a century.

History · 03

The Pakistan Movement, 1906–1940

From the Simla Deputation to the Lahore Resolution — Khilafat, the Nehru Report, and the Fourteen Points, taught as one connected argument.

History · 04

Jinnah, Iqbal & Rahmat Ali

Three men, three different Pakistans imagined — and the 14-mark question Cambridge loves to ask about who mattered most.

History · 05

Building a Nation, 1947–1971

Partition's first decade, Jinnah's death, the search for a constitution, and the separation of East Pakistan — handled with nuance, not slogans.

History · 06

Pakistan on the World Stage

Foreign relations since 1947: the US, China, India, Afghanistan and the Muslim world — the chapter that decides many final grades.

Geography · 07

Topography & The North

Karakoram, Himalaya, Hindu Kush. Plateaus, plains and deserts — and how to sketch them in the exam in under a minute.

Geography · 08

Climate & The Monsoon

Why the rain falls where it falls, what depressions in winter mean for wheat, and how to read every climate graph Cambridge can print.

Geography · 09

Water & The Indus System

Five rivers, one treaty, dozens of barrages. The water chapter taught along the river itself — Tarbela to Kotri.

Geography · 10

Agriculture: Cotton, Wheat & Rice

Crop by crop: inputs, seasons, problems, reforms. The backbone of Paper 2 — and of Pakistan's economy.

Geography · 11

Power & Industry

Hydel, thermal, nuclear, renewables — then into Karachi's steel, Faisalabad's textiles and the small-scale sector everyone underestimates.

Geography · 12

Trade, Transport & Population

Exports, ports and the motorway network, then the population pyramid — the final chapters before past-paper season begins.

How Classes Work

Built around the exam, not the textbook.

i.

Past-paper drilling

Every chapter ends in real Cambridge questions from the last fifteen years. You don't just learn the Lahore Resolution — you answer it, under timing, against the actual mark scheme.

ii.

Mark-scheme thinking

4-mark, 7-mark, 14-mark — each has a formula. Students learn to see the levels of response an examiner sees, so writing the answer becomes filling a structure, not guessing.

iii.

Maps & source analysis

Paper 2 is won on the map. Sketch maps, photographs, climate graphs and data tables are practised weekly until interpreting a source feels as natural as reading a sentence.

Track Record
